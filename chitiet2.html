M3 Max's Geekbench Score Is Equivalent to M2 Ultra on Mac Studio

K.I

2/11/2023 14:44 - Responses: 50

After Apple’s recent “Scary Fast” event, the thing I saw people discussing the most was the power of the M3 Series chip, because in reality, other than this chip, there was nothing else worth discussing in this event. Below is some information about the Geekbench performance score of the M3 Max chip.

First of all, the standard Apple M3 chip produces a Geekbench score of 3,076 points in single-core and 11,863 points in multi-core. As for the M3 Max, its Geekbench score of 2,971 points in single-core and 20,785 points in multi-core is almost equal to the M2 Ultra on the Mac Studio (2,692 points in single-core and 21,231 points in multi-core). Of course, this is only a part of the performance evaluation because there are some hardware that Geekbench cannot cover, such as the Media Engine system inside Apple Silicon chips. Therefore, the difference in actual performance depends on the actual usage of the user.

M3 Max chip score I am also someone who is quite interested in the performance of a MacBook in general, but I think the score for an Apple product has never been a decisive factor when users buy their products. Folklore can say: "When Apple releases new products, just buy them!", indeed, those who have decided to use MacBook for work always put their complete trust in the performance that MacBook brings and will not care much about such technical scores.
